How to boycott Google

Google Calendar is replaceable. They use a standardized calendar system that other products implement. Alternatives like Outlook, Apple Calendar, to-do list apps, and good 'ol pen and paper exist, and are well established. Personally, I will be switching to Apple Calendar, as it will sync with my iPhone, and is accessible through the web and on Apple products.

Other products are less replaceable. Although alternatives to Gmail and Drive exist, these products have so well seized the markets that it is impossible to function as a member of society without these products, among others. I will instead limit my use of these products, by using local text editors (like Word or Pages) and saving files locally.

Additionally, you can go even further by using alternatives to Google search, which is Google's second most profitable product. Google serves ads and promotes partners through their searches, and makes billions from this practice. By using alternatives like DuckDuckGo or Bing, you can cut off Google from the money they make from you.

Google's most profitable product is their advertisements and browser, Google Ads (in conjunction with Chrome). Their advertisements are used across the web, and by using an adblocker, notably uBlock Origin, you can refuse Google the priviledge of making money off our backs.

Despite Google's monopoly, we can make an impact. Use an adblocker, use DuckDuckGo, and if possible, switch to Firefox. Switch to alternatives to Google Calendar, Gmail, Drive, and other products. And spread the word. Google is not invulnerable, and we must speak up.
